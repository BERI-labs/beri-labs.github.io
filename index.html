What is BERI?

BERI is a framework that turns your school’s own documents into a clear, conversational way to find information. You can think of it like a focused search tool - like a specialised search engine - but instead of scanning the whole internet, it looks only at the policies and resources you choose to include.

When someone asks a question, BERI finds the most relevant parts of those documents and presents them as a simple, readable answer, with citations that link back to the original source so information can always be checked and verified.

Because it works from the materials you provide, each version of BERI is shaped around its environment. A school might use it as a policy assistant for staff, an information hub for prospective families, or a research companion for students. The aim isn’t to replace teaching or decision-making, but to make trusted information easier to navigate, especially in environments where clarity and accuracy matter.

Designed by students who understand the realities of school life, BERI focuses on accessibility, privacy, and flexibility. It runs locally and can be adapted to different roles and contexts, providing a structured yet customisable way to explore and understand complex information.

About

BERI was created by students who experienced first-hand how difficult it can be to find clear answers within complex school systems. Policies were often scattered across documents, important guidance was hard to locate

We wanted to build something that could turn existing resources into a conversational way to explore trusted information. Our vision is to make school systems easier to navigate, because schools should be a place to access information clearly and easily, so students and staff can focus on learning, and curiosity.
